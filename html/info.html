Functions of the Toolbox

1. Gives preset webpage color options

2. Sets the selected color as the webpage background

3. Sets the selected font color for all text on the webpage

4. Saturation slider for the webpage

5. Brightness slider for the webpage

6. Sets the selected font family for all text on the webpage

7. Increases & decreases the webpage's font size

8. Work in Progress! Presents alt text for images and generates captions for videos

9. Scans the webpage for any non ADA compliant features on the webpage

10. Selects option to download report after using the scan function

11. Saves altered webpage selections

12. Generates stored selections

13. Displays text for errors, reports, and data saved

14. Clears stored selections

15. Takes you to this page!

Purpose of the Toolbox

The Americans with Disabilities Act of 1990 - also known as the ADA - is a civil rights law
that was created with the purpose of prohibiting discrimination based on disability. Over the
years this act has also extended to creating guidelines that help promote accessibility for disabled
peoples in todayâ€™s society. A more recent development from the ADA are their guidelines on Web
Accessibility, and as most industries continue to embrace technology, it is important to ensure
that these new technologies are also made with accessibility in mind.

For our project we have decided to focus our attention on inaccessible
websites on the Internet for disabled people with visual and/or visual-related disabilities. These
disabilities can range from low vision to color blindness, as well as dyslexia and certain chronic
conditions, or others. Some people may be unable to read text that is in a specific font style, size,
or color. For others that may use screen readers, the user of certain fonts may be unreadable for them.
And those with eye sensitivities may have trouble reading from web pages with bright colors. Due to these
challenges, many disabled people can be left unable to access critical web content in an easy and quick manner.

We would like for our ADA Web Toolbox project to help disabled (and abled) people to make changes to the
web page they are viewing and promote a more accessible viewing experience. We also hope that this tool
will help make progress toward the goal of universal web accessibility across the board.


About the Team

Meet the development team - Julia, Haneen, and Karl. They are senior students
at the University of Michigan-Flint who were united by their shared passion to
advancing accessibility solutions. They have come together for their senior
capstone class to develop a Google extension geared towards enhancing and spreading
awareness about web accessibility.
